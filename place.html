About the Island

La Réunion is an overseas department of France located east of Madagascar in the Indian Ocean. Formed by volcanic activity, the island is dominated by two great peaks: the active Piton de la Fournaise and the extinct giant Piton des Neiges, along with three collapsed calderas known as cirques. Rainforest-covered mountains, black-sand beaches, and coral reefs give the island an extraordinary range of landscapes packed into a small territory.

Culture & Life

Réunionese culture blends French, African, Malagasy, Indian, and Chinese influences, visible in the island's Creole language, music, and cuisine. Dishes like cari (a Creole curry) and rougail saucisse are staples, and the island celebrates a lively Creole New Year. Hiking the cirques and climbing Piton de la Fournaise are among the most popular outdoor traditions, drawing visitors and locals alike into the volcanic interior.
